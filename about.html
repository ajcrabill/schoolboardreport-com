About School Board Report

Governance analysis grounded in what actually happens in actual meetings.

What We Do

School Board Report analyzes real school board decisions from actual meetings. Every piece of analysis we publish is grounded in documented decisions — board votes, meeting minutes, superintendent contracts, policy resolutions — from the previous 60 days. We do not analyze hypotheticals. We do not cover proposals that never came to a vote. We cover what boards actually did.

What We Cover

Our coverage focuses on three domains:

  1. Governance patterns — how boards exercise (and misexercise) their governance role
  2. Superintendent relationships — how board-superintendent dynamics shape district performance
  3. Board accountability — how boards hold themselves and district leadership accountable to student outcomes
Our Perspective

We analyze school board decisions through the lens of outcomes: do these decisions make it more or less likely that students will be well-served? We are not partisan and do not cover school boards through a political lens. We cover governance quality — the degree to which board decisions reflect sound governance practice — regardless of the political orientation of the board.

We do not cover every district or every meeting. We cover patterns that illuminate something about governance quality, and we cover decisions that have consequences worth tracing.

Publication Schedule

New analysis is posted Monday, Wednesday, and Friday. We cover meetings that occurred in the previous 60 days. We do not publish real-time coverage — our value is analysis, not speed.

Who We Write For
  • School board members who want to understand governance best practices grounded in real examples
  • Superintendents navigating board relationships
  • Researchers and policy professionals tracking governance trends
  • Community members who want to understand what their board is actually doing
