Districts in Recent Coverage

School districts featured in School Board Report analysis over the past 60 days.

School Board Report covers districts where board decisions illustrate important governance patterns. Coverage does not imply dysfunction — some districts appear because they exemplify effective governance practice. We cover what boards actually decided, and analyze what it means.

Houston Independent School District
Texas
Featured in
Three Districts, Three Different Approaches to the Same Governance Challenge
June 9, 2026
Houston ISD's board repeatedly intervened in operational decisions before the Texas Education Agency assumed oversight in 2023. The TEA intervention followed documented patterns of board micromanagement that eroded the superintendent's ability to lead. Houston ISD is now one of the most-studied cases in school board governance literature.
Jefferson County School District — Jeffco
Colorado
Featured in
Three Districts, Three Different Approaches to the Same Governance Challenge
June 9, 2026
Jeffco's board fired Superintendent Cindy Stevenson in 2014 following a board election that shifted the board's majority. The dismissal, widely covered nationally, triggered a teacher strike and months of community disruption. Jeffco has since stabilized under subsequent board compositions.
Wake County Public School System
North Carolina
Featured in
Three Districts, Three Different Approaches to the Same Governance Challenge
June 9, 2026
Wake County has experienced both constructive and dysfunctional board-superintendent relationships across different board compositions, making it a useful case study in how board culture — not just policy — shapes governance quality.
Providence Public School District
Rhode Island
Featured in
The Quiet Pattern Behind Boards That Overstep
June 4, 2026
The Rhode Island Department of Education assumed oversight of Providence Public Schools in 2019 following documented findings of governance and operational failures. Providence is a case study in how governance problems compound over time before triggering external intervention.
Camden City School District
New Jersey
Featured in
The Quiet Pattern Behind Boards That Overstep
June 4, 2026
New Jersey assumed state control of Camden City Schools following a period of documented board governance problems. Like Providence, Camden illustrates how early warning signs of board dysfunction often go unaddressed until intervention becomes unavoidable.
